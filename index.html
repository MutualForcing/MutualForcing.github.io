Abstract

We present Mutual Forcing, a novel dual-mode self-evolution framework for fast auto-regressive audio-video character generation. Our approach enables high-fidelity, temporally coherent generation across multiple domains — including singing, background music synchronization, multi-person dialogue, animal sounds, and long-form video synthesis — without domain-specific fine-tuning.

The core insight of Mutual Forcing lies in a bidirectional forcing mechanism between audio and video modalities, where each modality iteratively refines the other through a self-evolving dual-mode schedule. This allows the model to achieve strong cross-domain generalization while maintaining generation speed competitive with single-pass methods.

Extensive experiments demonstrate that our framework generalizes across diverse real-world scenarios, producing results with natural prosody, accurate lip sync, and semantically aligned background music — all from a unified model.

Method Overview

Mutual Forcing introduces a dual-mode self-evolution loop between the audio and video generation streams. At each auto-regressive step, the two modes force each other through a learned coupling, progressively aligning temporal, semantic, and prosodic features across modalities.
